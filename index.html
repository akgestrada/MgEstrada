IPhone 14 Pro Max

The lates iPhone boasts an extensive array of advancements, which may not be groundbreaking to all users but contribute to a compelling package- exceptionally bright screen with AoD, remedy for the irritating notch, and all-around camera system with superior full-resolution imagery. These innovations are complemented by the Pro Max's staple features, such as remarkable battery life, exceptional speakers, and a robust and high-quality build. Another drawcard is the most powerful chipset available in the market, along with a minimum of five years of software support, making it a strong contender for long-term iPhone enthusiasts. In conclusion, if you are contemplating an upgrade and the price aligns with your budget, the iPhone 14 Pro Max is an excellent choice with an alluring combination of features.

Samsung S23 Ultra

The 2023 flagship Galaxy smartphone is a device of unparalleled quality and reliability, with a single drawback that's hardly worth mentioning. This year's Galaxy S23 Ultra isn't necessarily revolutionary, which could be viewed as a negative. However, that perspective is short-sighted; the S22 Ultra was already an outstanding device, and the S23 Ultra builds upon its predecessor's strengths. While the absence of revolutionary features may be seen as a disadvantage, it's critical to keep things in perspective. The S23 Ultra is a mature, well-crafted device that has a great deal to offer. It's an excellent option for individuals seeking a dependable and high-performing smartphone. Therefore, the Galaxy S23 Ultra is undoubtedly a highly recommended device.

Google Pixel 6a

The Pixel 6a by Google seems to have nailed the A Series format, packing in the premium Pixel experience into an affordable option that's hard to pass up. The slightly lower refresh rate display and slow charging times are minor drawbacks when weighed against the overall quality. Offering the Google Tensor over last year's Snapdragon 765G at the same Php 20,000 price point is a great deal. The camera still performs well, the software and performance are well polished, and the battery life is commendable. Additionally, Google has resolved performance throttling issues with the Pixel 6a's Google Tensor. All in all, it's one of Google's most competitive smartphones in recent times, and we wholeheartedly recommend it.

Google Pixel 7

The Pixel 7 is a noteworthy smartphone within the Php 30,000 - 35,000 range. It delivers flagship-level performance, although not quite up to the usual standards. It's undoubtedly one of the top camera phones, and features an OLED screen with excellent stereo speakers, a long-lasting battery life for its size, and state-of-the-art software providing frequent updates and advanced features. However, there are some limitations to bear in mind - no authentic zoom camera, display is capped at 90Hz, some software functionalities depend on the location, and charging method is significantly outdated for a 2022 flagship. The Pixel 7 offers a unique blend of a top-notch camera experience, long battery life, and AI-based features, making it the smartest choice available at that price point.
