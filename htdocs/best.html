IPhone 14 Pro Max

Php 77,000

The latest iteration of the iPhone boasts an extensive array of advancements, which may not be groundbreaking or relevant to individual users but collectively contribute to a compelling package. These include an exceptionally bright screen with AoD, a remedy for the irritating notch, an all-encompassing camera system with superior full-resolution imagery, and emergency hardware/software functionalities.

These innovations are complemented by the Pro Max's staple features, such as remarkable battery life, exceptional speakers, and a robust and high-quality build. Another drawcard is the most powerful chipset available in the market, along with a minimum of five years of software support, making it a strong contender for long-term iPhone enthusiasts.

However, the cost escalation in RoW markets (excluding the US) can be challenging to accept on various fronts. On one hand, it involves a direct monetary impact, while on the other, it raises concerns about subsidizing US customers' purchases. Nonetheless, the iPhone 14 Pro Max is a remarkable device with minimal shortcomings, and any perceived drawbacks are not unique to this generation or size.

In conclusion, if you are contemplating an upgrade and the price aligns with your budget, the iPhone 14 Pro Max is an excellent choice with an alluring combination of features.

Samsung S23 Ultra

Php 90,000

The 2023 flagship Galaxy smartphone is a device of unparalleled quality and reliability, with a single drawback that's hardly worth mentioning. This year's Galaxy S23 Ultra isn't necessarily revolutionary, which could be viewed as a negative. However, that perspective is short-sighted; the S22 Ultra was already an outstanding device, and the S23 Ultra builds upon its predecessor's strengths. The improvements may not elicit gasps of astonishment, but they are certainly worth noting.

For starters, the battery life is enhanced, and the charging speed, although only slightly faster than Apple or Google, is still quite impressive. Additionally, the display is one of the best in the industry, while the camera is improved beyond the impressive 200MP figure displayed in promotional materials. These characteristics, along with the inclusion of the S Pen, make the S23 Ultra one of the most versatile and user-friendly smartphones on the market.

While the absence of revolutionary features may be seen as a disadvantage, it's critical to keep things in perspective. The S23 Ultra is a mature, well-crafted device that has a great deal to offer. It's an excellent option for individuals seeking a dependable and high-performing smartphone. Therefore, the Galaxy S23 Ultra is undoubtedly a highly recommended device.

Google Pixel 7

Php 29,000

The Pixel 7 is a noteworthy smartphone within the Php 30,000 - 35,000 range. It delivers flagship-level performance, although not quite up to the usual standards. It's undoubtedly one of the top camera phones, and features an OLED screen with excellent stereo speakers, a long-lasting battery life (considering its size), and state-of-the-art software providing frequent updates and advanced features.

However, there are some limitations to bear in mind. There is no authentic zoom camera, the display is capped at 90Hz, some software functionalities depend on your location, and the charging method is significantly outdated for a 2022 flagship release.

All in all, the Pixel 7, along with the Pixel 7 Pro, is the best Google smartphone. And that is no small feat, considering Google's smartphone release history. Fortunately, the Pixel 7 is not one of those flops.

Therefore, do we suggest it? Absolutely! The Pixel 7 offers a unique blend of a top-notch camera experience, long battery life, and AI-based features, making it the smartest choice available at that price point.

Vivo X80 Pro

Php 60,000

The latest addition to vivo's smartphone lineup, the X80 Pro, boasts top-notch camera hardware that snaps remarkable photos and records impressive videos. Along with its impressive display, it also charges up quickly, although its battery life falls short of expectations. Its stereo speakers are among the best in the market, and its sizable ultrasonic fingerprint reader is a pleasure to use. The design is also a plus, even if the sleek back may not offer the best grip.

However, the X80 Pro's performance is marred by significant heat buildup and thermal throttling. On the other hand, the limited display refresh rate in gaming could turn this negative into a positive.

Nevertheless, the true standout feature of the X80 Pro is its quad-camera setup, making it a rare find among cameraphones with such superb capabilities. While the Xiaomi 12 Ultra may eventually surpass it, that model doesn't exist yet, and the Galaxy's 10x zoom may be the gamechanger for some. But for a well-rounded, superior camera experience, the X80 Pro is an easy recommendation. It also helps that it's a solid phone overall.

Google Pixel 6a

Php 21,000

The Pixel 6a by Google seems to have nailed the A Series format, packing in the premium Pixel experience into an affordable option that's hard to pass up. The slightly lower refresh rate display and slow charging times are minor drawbacks when weighed against the overall quality.

Offering the Google Tensor over last year's Snapdragon 765G at the same Php 20,000 price point is a great deal. The camera still performs well, the software and performance are well polished, and the battery life is commendable. Additionally, Google has resolved performance throttling issues with the Pixel 6a's Google Tensor.

All in all, it's one of Google's most competitive smartphones in recent times, and we wholeheartedly recommend it. However, it's important to note that things may differ in India, where consumers have a plethora of options in this price range, and the Pixel 6a's higher cost may make it less appealing.

Sony Xperia 1 IV

Php 62,000

The new Sony Xperia smartphone offers impressive features, including a 4K OLED screen, the latest Snapdragon chipset, front-facing stereo speakers, and an excellent battery. The standout feature, however, is its professional camera kit, which boasts excellent processing and one of the best daylight photo qualities on the market. The Cinema Pro and Video Pro apps also allow for high-quality filmmaking on the go.

Unfortunately, the Xperia has its drawbacks. The phone suffers from heavy throttling, with the screen refresh rate dropping to 60fps and processing power being halved when the phone gets too hot. This can cause issues when shooting for extended periods. Additionally, the video capturing process is scattered across multiple apps, making it overly complicated, and the lack of Night Mode is disappointing.

Despite these flaws, the Sony Xperia is still an appealing option, especially for camera professionals. Sony may address the throttling issues in future models, and the phone's overall potential makes it worth considering. Those who find it too complicated or cannot overlook its shortcomings may want to explore other options.
